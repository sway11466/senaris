SUBJECT: A skeleton — the basic rank-and-file foot soldier of the undead horde, and the visual anchor of this faction. A walking human skeleton: bleached bone white-grey bones, a bare skull with empty eye sockets lit by a COLD BLUE-GREEN glow (the sign that the corpse moves). Its gear is what it wore in life, now rotted: cracked dark leather straps, a tattered scrap of cloth at the waist, a few tarnished and rusted metal fittings — this is once-proper equipment left to decay, NOT scavenged junk. Grave-earth stains on the bones. It holds a rusty one-handed sword upright and ready in front of the body. Bony and fragile-looking, but sharp and quietly menacing — not comical, not cute. Keep the mature, muted, limited palette: bone white-grey, rot brown, rusted metal, with the cold blue-green glow ONLY in the eye sockets (small and contained). NOT bright cartoon colors.
