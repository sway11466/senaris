SUBJECT: Cover art for the campaign "Goblin Raid". At dusk, on a narrow dirt road between rocky slopes just outside a small town, a tiny band of steel-blue soldiers holds the line against a goblin horde. In the foreground a human fighter in steel plate armor with a blue tunic grips his one-handed sword (NO shield), standing protectively in front of a novice swordsman — a young adult rookie soldier in lighter gear, slightly shorter and leaner than the fighter, holding a smaller sword in both hands, nervous but determined; clearly a green recruit, NOT a child. Facing them, the opposite side and the deep background swarm with goblins — sickly olive-green skin, long pointed ears, sly yellow eyes glinting from the shadows, crude chipped blades and a bent short bow — and behind the mob loom the silhouettes of a hulking hobgoblin with a crude two-handed axe and the goblin lord wearing a jagged rusty iron crown. Outnumbered but resolute: warm low light on the two heroes, cool shadow over the horde, steel-blue against sickly green.
